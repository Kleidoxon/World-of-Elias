CRAFT NOTE — THE WORLD'S NAME
Naming a world "Elias" is a quiet but powerful choice. It sounds human — almost intimate, almost biographical — rather than grand and invented. This creates an immediate narrative question: who was Elias? A founding god? A dead mage? A sleeping king? Leaving that answer unresolved until the right moment is a significant storytelling asset.

Recommendation: Elias was the First Weaver — a mage-god who shaped the world from raw chaos and then vanished, either dying, ascending, or simply walking away. The world carries his name like a gravestone. Every province interprets his legacy differently. That disagreement is itself political.


◈ I. THE WEAVE & MAGIC SYSTEM
THE WEAVE & MANA — FOUNDATION
Magic in Elias flows from the Weave — the fabric Elias the First Weaver used to shape existence into being. The Weave is not a force external to the world; it is the world's underlying structure, invisible to most, felt by the rare few.

To use magic, a person must enter a spiritual state through meditation, accessing the Weave's threads and drawing them inward as mana. Mana is the fuel. Its capacity (how much the body can hold) and quality (how refined and powerful it is) determine the ceiling of everything a mage can do.
MANA ACQUISITION — THREE STEPS
Step 1 — Meditation & Spiritual State: The caster achieves stillness of mind sufficient to perceive the Weave. In crisis, experienced mages drop into a partial spiritual state in seconds. Novices may need minutes. The state cannot be forced — it must be entered willingly. This is why panic, extreme grief, and overwhelming rage are dangerous to untrained mages: the Weave responds to uncontrolled emotion with uncontrolled output.

Step 2 — Pulling the Thread: Once in the spiritual state, the caster grasps a Weave thread and draws it inward. The thread converts to mana upon contact with the caster's inner reservoir. Success rate depends on the caster's Inner Trait and Potential Mana Level (both listed in full below).

Step 3 — Storing & Casting: Mana is stored in the body's Weave-reservoir — a metaphysical structure, not physical; it cannot be surgically found or destroyed by conventional means. The reservoir refills through further meditation or rest in Weave-dense areas (near ley-lines, old forests, volcanic Weave-nodes).

Formation: Black-fire Stone is crystallised Weave energy — Weave threads that became trapped in mineral formations over millions of years during periods of intense magical activity in Elias's geological past. The Extraction Problem — The Fraying: When Black-fire Stone is extracted at industrial scale, the Weave threads within it are severed. Each severed thread sends a fracture propagating outward along connected Weave fabric — like a pulled thread in woven cloth. The fracture radiates outward at approximately 3 kilometres per year.

Single or small-scale extraction: negligible effect on the overall Weave fabric. Solrath's current imperial industrial extraction rate: catastrophic.

The Aelindra Spire's deep archive (Archive Seven, restricted access) has mapped the fracture spread using 14 months of Ley-Scout field data. At current rates, in approximately 23 years, the fraying will reach the foundational Weave nodes — the deep threads that hold Elias's physical laws stable. What happens then is uncertain. The scholars' best working hypothesis involves the word unravelling. They have not published this finding. They are not sure who to tell. They are not sure telling anyone would change anything.

Why Solrath's Deposits Are Uniquely Dangerous: Solrath's Black-fire is the oldest in Elias — formed during the original act of Weaving by Elias himself. These are not peripheral threads in the fabric. They are foundational. Extracting them is not pulling loose threads from the edge. It is pulling the threads from the centre of the weave. The structural consequence is proportionally worse.

The Magic Circle: At Grand Master tier and above, active casting generates a visible magic circle directly beneath the mage's feet — a Weave-pattern specific to their bloodline and magic school, glowing in their bloodline colour. Circle complexity increases with tier. At Omnipotent tier, the circle is architectural in intricacy, visible from hundreds of metres in daylight, and causes lesser mages nearby to physically feel the Weave vibrate — described as a low pressure in the chest, like standing too close to a struck bell.

Bullet Interaction:

Standard (non-enhanced) bullet: stopped by Adept (IV)+ ward
Black-fire powder enhanced or Weave-enchanted bullet: requires Expert (VI)+ ward
Grand Master (VIII) and above: ambient Weave-field passively deflects standard rounds without a raised ward
At Godly (IX)+: the mage's presence distorts space slightly around them; standard ammunition curves away on approach. Enhanced ammunition still requires active deflection.

CRAFT NOTE — Guns & Magic: The coexistence of firearms and magic is the most interesting mechanical tension. A mage who needs seconds to raise a ward dies to a soldier who pulls a trigger in one. This makes conventional soldiers genuinely dangerous to mages — and drives the political fear that mages have of imperial gun battalions. Thurak's military doctrine trains riflemen specifically to break mage concentration. The moment a bullet clips a mage mid-cast, the Weave thread they're holding snaps back. What happens then is never pretty.

Special Rules
Light vs. Dark at equal tier: Both collapse into a grey explosion of raw Weave energy — destructive to both casters and their immediate surroundings. Bystanders within radius experience temporary loss of Weave access (hours to days depending on proximity and their own tier).

Dark magic sustained use: Amplifies negative emotions in both caster and nearby targets. Without Resolve or Order as a balancing Inner Trait, sustained Dark casting causes slow corruption of the caster's mana — visible as black veins threading through their bloodline glow colour. Advanced corruption leaves permanent marks.

Chaos vs. Chaos at equal tier: Genuinely unpredictable. Both casters and all bystanders within range are at equal risk. No mage, regardless of tier, can fully model the outcome of two Chaos casters in open conflict. Even Omnipotent-tier mages approach this scenario with caution.

Light magic and Void: Light cannot exist in a space where Void has been actively applied. The Void must be broken or allowed to expire before Light magic functions in that area.

Raw Chaos contamination (non-Godly exposure): A non-Godly person who attempts to pull Raw Chaos and fails experiences one of three outcomes:

Tier VII–VIII: Temporary psychosis (hours to weeks), mana corruption (recoverable with treatment)
Tier IV–VI: Permanent mana corruption, possible loss of casting ability entirely
Tier I–III: Near-certain death or permanent incapacitation


◈ II. THE POLITICAL LANDSCAPE
THE FIVE FACTIONS
CRAFT NOTE — Political Structure: Elias is governed — loosely, fractiously — by the Imperial Throne in Valdris. In theory, the Throne rules all eleven provinces through a system of appointed Governors and elected Provincial Councils. In practice, five power blocs have made that theory increasingly fictional.



1. THE IMPERIAL THRONE (Valdris — Centre) "The Weave holds all threads. So too the Throne."

The oldest institution in Elias. The current Emperor is Cassian IV — intelligent, ageing, and increasingly surrounded by advisors whose interests diverge sharply from his own. The Throne controls the largest single military force, the imperial tax apparatus, and the licensing of all commercial magic use. Its weakness: it is over-extended. Eleven provinces are too many for one centre to hold when four of them are pulling outward simultaneously.

CRAFT NOTE: The most dramatically interesting emperors are not evil — they are tired and right about the wrong things. Cassian IV should believe, genuinely, that the Throne is the only thing standing between Elias and civil war. He is partially correct. That partial correctness makes him dangerous and tragic simultaneously.



2. THE MERCHANT COMPACT (Seranthos + Lyrannis + Keldrim) "Coin flows where walls do not."

An economic alliance, not an ideological one. Three provinces whose prosperity depends on trade routes that the imperial tariff system actively hinders. The Compact does not want to destroy the empire — it wants to renegotiate the terms of membership. Their leverage: Keldrim controls the only reliable mountain crossing between east and west Elias. Lyrannis controls the sea lanes. Seranthos holds two-thirds of the empire's banking reserves. Together, they could strangle the imperial economy without firing a single shot.

CRAFT NOTE: The Compact is the faction most readers will initially sympathise with — reasonable people who want fair trade. This makes them ideal for a midpoint betrayal. Someone within the Compact is willing to go much further than mere renegotiation. The question is who, and what they're willing to sacrifice to get there.



3. THE MAGE COUNCIL OF AELINDRA (Aelindra — Northern Highlands) "The Weave answers to no throne."

The oldest magical institution in Elias, predating the empire by four centuries. The Council does not seek political power in the conventional sense — it seeks independence from political power. Imperial law requires all mages to register, pay a licensing tithe, and submit to inspection. The Council regards this as an obscenity. They have complied, nominally, for a generation. The compliance is ending.

What drives their urgency: a discovery in the deep archives of Aelindra Spire — something about the true nature of the Weave and the cost of what the empire is doing to Black-fire Stone in Solrath — that the Council has not yet made public. [PLOT NOTE P-02]

CRAFT NOTE: The Mage Council's secret is the most powerful narrative slow-burn. Don't reveal what they found too early. Let it surface in fragments — a scholar who drinks too much and says too much; a Spire library whose certain section is always locked; a mage who left the Council under circumstances no one discusses. The reader should piece it together before it's stated. When it's finally stated, it should recontextualise everything that came before.



4. THE EMBER COMPACT (Solrath + The Ashen Hand of Morvaine) "What was taken by fire will be returned by fire."

The most radical faction. Born from two separate grievances that found each other: Solrath's indigenous population, whose land sits atop Black-fire Stone deposits that the empire extracts and profits from while Solrath itself starves; and Morvaine's industrial workers — The Ashen Hand — who produce the empire's iron, its guns, its machinery, and cannot afford to eat the food they see shipped past their doors to Valdris.

The Ember Compact does not want to renegotiate. It wants to burn the current structure to ash and build something new on the scorched earth.

CRAFT NOTE: The Ember Compact needs a leader who is genuinely right about the injustice and genuinely terrifying in their methods. The moral weight of the novel depends on not making the revolutionaries cartoonishly evil. They are correct. They are also willing to do things that the reader will find unconscionable. That gap is the story.



5. THE GRAY LEGION (Thurak — Southern Frontier) "Victory is the only argument that does not require a translator."

General Varek Thurn has commanded Thurak's military for eleven years. He is the most effective military mind in Elias. He is loyal to the empire — and he is watching the empire slowly fail. His own loyalty is becoming a liability. The Gray Legion controls the best-equipped conventional army in Elias. If Thurn decides to act rather than wait, the map changes overnight. [PLOT NOTE P-05]

CRAFT NOTE: Thurn should not appear until he matters. His absence from early chapters should be felt as weight — other characters reference him, fear him, seek to secure his loyalty. When he finally appears on the page, the reader should already understand what it means that this specific man is in this specific room.

Political Character: The most loyal province to the Throne — because it is the Throne. The nobles of Valdris have the most to lose if the empire fragments. Their loyalty is self-interest wearing the coat of patriotism.

Political Character: Keldrim's power is leverage, not force. The Stonegate Guild holds the passes — which means every army that wants to move from east to west has to negotiate with them. Magic: Earth and stone magic (Stonebinding) — passive, architectural, generational. Keldrim's mages don't cast spells in the conventional sense; they speak to stone and stone listens. The Guild has Stonebinders embedded in every pass fortification, maintaining wards that have stood for four hundred years.

CRAFT NOTE: Keldrim is the political wildcard. In the endgame of any faction conflict, who controls the passes determines who wins. Consider a scene where two faction representatives both arrive at Keldrim Peak to negotiate passage rights — and the Guild Master calmly plays them against each other in the same meeting room, over tea. The Stonegate Guild has been doing this for centuries. They are very good at it.

Magic: Animist — Forestbinding. The Vethara's relationship with the Weave is not academic; it is ecological. They do not manipulate the Weave so much as are part of it.
